POS Register

Follow one till session end to end — open the register with a cash float, ring up sales, take payment, then close the drawer. POS resolves inventory in real time on every sale, but accumulates the day's money inside the SHIFT and posts ONE aggregated Z-entry at close, routed by payment method. Captured live: open → two sales (cash + bKash) → count the drawer → the balanced journal entry in Accounting.

1

STEP 01·NAVIGATE

Open the register — count the starting float

A till session begins by OPENING the register. Count the physical cash already in the drawer (the opening float — tap a quick amount or key it on the numpad) and hit Open Register. This starts the SHIFT: from here every sale accumulates into this session's running payment breakdown, and the float becomes the baseline the drawer is reconciled against at close. Nothing sells until a shift is open.

2

STEP 02·ORIENT

The terminal: one branch, one open shift

The register is now live: a product grid with per-branch stock and prices on the left, the cart on the right. Two things scope everything. First the BRANCH — 'Eternal Ceramics HQ' here — the till is bound to one store (organizationId = branchId), so it only ever sells and decrements THAT branch's stock. Second the SHIFT: the 'Shift Open' badge means the accumulator is running, and every sale books into its payment breakdown — not the ledger, yet.

3

STEP 03·CLICK

Ring up a sale — scan or tap

Scan a barcode (F4), search (F3), or tap a product to add it to the cart. The line, its quantity stepper, and the running Total update live and VAT-inclusive. Each line is checked against the branch's live Flow quant, so the till cannot oversell what is physically on the shelf — the same on-hand number Stock Levels shows, read at the point of sale. Optionally attach a customer, then Checkout.

4

STEP 04·RESOLVE

Paying resolves INVENTORY immediately (per sale)

The instant a sale completes, inventory resolves in real time in this branch's Flow context: a move runs from the stock location to the customer location, the quant decrements, and the cost layers consumed post COGS — Dr COGS (5111) / Cr Inventory (1164). Stock and cost are never deferred; only the REVENUE side waits for shift close. That keeps on-hand accurate to the second even though the sales JE is batched.

5

STEP 05·RESOLVE

The tender METHOD is recorded — not posted yet

Choose how the customer pays — a single method, or Split across several (cash + card + bKash). Each tender is added to the shift's paymentBreakdown BY METHOD. This is the field that later decides which cash or bank GL account gets debited. Critically, paying posts NOTHING to the ledger on its own: the money just accumulates in the open shift, method by method, all day. It also creates a per-sale revenue transaction (visible on the Transactions screen, tagged source=pos) — but the JE waits.

6

STEP 06·RESULT

Sale complete — receipt ready, drawer updated

The sale confirms with an order number and total; print or reprint the receipt, then New Sale (F2) for the next customer. Stock is already down, the tender is banked into the shift, a POS transaction exists — everything except the day's journal entry, which is deliberately deferred. A busy store rings hundreds of these; a JE per sale would bury the ledger, so they batch.

7

STEP 07·CLICK

Close the register — count & reconcile the drawer

End of day, open the register menu → Close Register. The closing screen freezes the shift: Sales count, total Revenue, and EXPECTED cash (opening float + cash sales − cash refunds — note bKash/card are NOT in the drawer). Key the physically counted cash; any gap is recorded as an over/short on the shift. Here it is an Exact Match. Confirming Close Register is the moment the ledger gets written.

8

STEP 08·RESOLVE

Close posts ONE Daily-Z, routed per method

At close the ledger bridge (onShiftClosed) builds a SINGLE journal entry from the frozen paymentBreakdown. For each method with net positive sales it debits that method's clearing/cash account — cash → Cash in Hand (1111), mobile money → Mobile Money Merchant (1126), card → Gateway Clearing (1125). Here two tenders → two debit lines, 17,950 each. Routing each method to its own account lets daily settlement reconcile clearing → bank as payouts land. Idempotent per shift (key pos-shift-{id}), so a double-close is a no-op.

9

STEP 09·RESULT

Balanced & filed — Accounting → Journal Entries

The posted entry lives in Accounting → Journal Entries, labelled 'POS Daily Sales — {branch} — {date}', reference POS_SALES/YYYY/MM/####. The per-method debits credit a single Sales Revenue (4111) line net of tax, plus Output VAT (2132) when applicable — Total Debit = Total Credit, Balanced. That net revenue and VAT roll into the very same Mushak 9.1 return as ecom sales: POS and online are two front-ends onto one set of books. One entry, per method, for the whole day.
