Warehouse Loading & Catalog Link

The procure-to-pay guide showed WHAT a receipt posts to the ledger. This one shows WHERE the goods physically land and how they become sellable. Follow the received stock as it loads into a warehouse location, becomes an on-hand quant, and links back — by SKU — to a catalog product whose storefront buy-button reflects it automatically. This is the join between purchasing, the WMS, and the catalog.

STEP 01·RESOLVE

Every branch is a warehouse node

A receipt doesn't land in 'the warehouse' abstractly — it lands at a specific NODE. On first use each branch auto-provisions one warehouse node plus four locations: stock (your real on-hand shelf), vendor (virtual — where purchases come FROM), customer (virtual — where sales go TO), and adjustment (virtual — for counts and write-offs). Physical and virtual locations are the same primitive; that symmetry is why every move is a clean double-entry of stock.

2

STEP 02·RESOLVE

Receiving is a move INTO the stock location

When you received the PO, the Flow move ran from the supplier's VENDOR location into the STOCK location shown here. Quant is tracked PER LOCATION — that is how the system distinguishes goods physically on your shelf from goods in-transit on a transfer, or units removed by an adjustment. Load-in is literally 'increment the quant at the stock location'.

3

STEP 03·NAVIGATE

Stock Levels reads the quant

Stock Levels is a live read of the Flow quant for this branch's stock location: units on hand, and available (on-hand minus any cart/order reservations), per SKU. Scan a barcode or type a SKU to jump straight to an item. Nothing is stored twice — this screen is the WMS quant, surfaced.

4

STEP 04·RESOLVE

The catalog links to stock by SKU

How does a catalog Product know its warehouse stock? The CatalogBridge maps each variant's SKU (or, for simple products, the product _id) to a skuRef — the exact same key Flow stores the quant under. So a product and its on-hand are joined by skuRef, not by a fragile foreign key. Search the catalog here; every SKU you see is a live handle into the WMS.

5

STEP 05·RESOLVE

Stock projection feeds the storefront

The public storefront never queries Flow live on every page view. Instead each Product carries a stockProjection cache — head-office on-hand — rebuilt automatically whenever a quant changes: a move commits, a reservation is consumed or released, an adjustment posts, or a PROCUREMENT is RECEIVED. That last one closes the loop: receiving this PO at head office propagates, event-driven, all the way to the online buy-button. Sub-branch stock stays POS-only and never oversells the storefront.
