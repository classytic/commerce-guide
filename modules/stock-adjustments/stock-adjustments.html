Stock Adjustments & Cycle Count

Procurement adds stock, sales remove it, transfers move it — yet the physical shelf still drifts from the system through theft, breakage and miscounts. Stock Audit is the CORRECTION leg that closes that gap. It reconciles counted-against-system, and every variance resolves into a Flow move to the adjustment location AND a journal entry. But uniquely, that JE lands as a DRAFT — because writing inventory value off the balance sheet is a decision a human must approve, not an automatic post.

STEP 01·NAVIGATE

Three depths of count

Stock Audit offers three count scopes: Spot Check (a handful of SKUs), Cycle Count (a rotating subset so the whole catalogue is counted over time without ever closing the store), and Full Count (everything, for year-end). Each opens a count session (CNT-…) that freezes the system's expected on-hand at that moment and gives you a sheet to enter the physical count against.

2

STEP 02·RESOLVE

A count reconciles against the live quant

The expected column is the live Flow quant — the exact on-hand number Stock Levels shows. You enter what you physically counted; the difference is the variance. If counted equals expected, nothing posts — a clean count is a no-op. Only a variance triggers the resolution below, so the ledger is never touched for stock that was already correct.

3

STEP 03·RESOLVE

Variance resolves INVENTORY via the adjustment location

A shortfall runs a Flow move from the stock location into the ADJUSTMENT location — the fourth virtual location every branch is provisioned with, exactly for this. An overage moves the other way. Either way the quant is corrected to match the physical count. Unlike a sale, the goods don't go to a customer; they leave (or arrive) through the adjustment sink, which is what keeps every stock change a clean, auditable double-entry of location.

4

STEP 04·RESOLVE

...and resolves the LEDGER by direction

The direction of the variance picks the journal entry. A LOSS (shrinkage, theft, breakage) posts Dr Inventory Shrinkage (6711, a P&L expense) / Cr Merchandise (1164) — shown here, a BDT 100,000 damaged write-off. A GAIN (found stock, provision write-back) posts Dr Merchandise (1164) / Cr Inventory Gain — Other Income (4317). A gain must NEVER credit 6711 to 'reverse' shrinkage: crediting an expense account gives it a negative balance and silently inflates net income. Loss is an expense; gain is other income — two different accounts on purpose.

5

STEP 05·RESOLVE

The adjustment JE posts as a DRAFT for review

Look at the state: draft. This is the defining difference from every other flow in this suite. A receipt or a sale AUTO-posts; a stock adjustment does NOT. Writing value off the balance sheet is a judgement call, so the entry is created in draft with its variance and reason, and finance reviews and posts it deliberately. That single design choice is the control that stops a fat-fingered count from silently vaporising inventory value.

6

STEP 06·RESOLVE

Posted variances flow to shrinkage KPI + valuation

Once posted, account 6711 accumulates your shrinkage rate — a real KPI finance watches on the P&L — and the corrected quant re-values inventory at its cost layers. If the count was at head office, the corrected on-hand also re-syncs the storefront projection so the online buy-button reflects reality. A count is not bookkeeping hygiene; it keeps stock valuation, COGS margins, and the public storefront all honest against the physical shelf.
