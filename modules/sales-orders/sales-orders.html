Sales Orders

The mirror image of procure-to-pay. A sales order is a customer's intent to buy; placing it only RESERVES stock. Fulfilment is the resolution — it moves inventory OUT, consumes cost layers into COGS, and books revenue, output VAT, and a receivable in one stroke. Follow an order from placed to delivered and watch six ledger effects fall out of a single fulfil action.

1

STEP 01·NAVIGATE

Orders is the sell-side lifecycle

Every customer order for the active branch, with its payment and fulfilment status. Placing an order RESERVES the units — a soft hold where available on-hand drops but nothing has physically left the shelf. When money hits the ledger depends on terms: a prepaid/POS sale books revenue when paid, whereas a COD order books the receivable and revenue at placement (Dr A/R 1141) because the courier collects later. Either way COGS waits for fulfilment. Cart abandonment releases the hold and re-syncs the storefront projection.

2

STEP 02·CLICK

Open the order — Payment, Delivery, Shipping

The detail sheet splits the order into its lifecycle: Payment (was it collected — prepaid, or COD to reconcile later), Delivery/Shipping (how it leaves), and the line items + totals. Each transition here is a trigger that resolves into stock and the ledger — the buttons are the least of it.

3

STEP 03·CLICK

Ship / fulfil the order

Ship Order is the moment intent becomes a transaction. It is one click, but it fires the two resolutions below — inventory and ledger — atomically, so stock and books can never disagree about a sale.

4

STEP 04·RESOLVE

Fulfilment resolves INVENTORY out

Flow runs a move from the STOCK location to the CUSTOMER (virtual) location: the quant decrements, the earlier reservation is CONSUMED, and the specific COST LAYERS the units draw from (FIFO/AVCO) are what set the cost of the sale. This is why receiving opened cost layers — selling closes them. Available and on-hand both drop; the storefront projection re-syncs.

5

STEP 05·RESOLVE

The sale posts TWO separate journal entries

A sale never nets cost and price into one entry — they are two JEs so the P&L stays honest. (1) COST, on fulfilment: Dr COGS (5111) / Cr Inventory (1164) at the cost-layer value consumed above. (2) PRICE: Cr Sales Revenue (4111) + Cr Output VAT (2132), and the DEBIT routes by how the customer pays — card → Gateway Clearing (1125, held by the gateway until payout), bKash/Nagad → Mobile Money (1126), cash → Petty Cash (1111), and COD → Accounts Receivable (1141), because the courier collects and remits later. Inventory leaves at cost, revenue books at price; the gap is gross margin. A promo posts as a Dr Sales Discount (4115) contra-revenue line so gross sales stay visible in the trial balance rather than being silently netted off.

6

STEP 06·RESOLVE

Revenue flows to VAT + receivables

The output VAT credited to 2132 is the same figure that rolls into the Mushak 9.1 monthly VAT return, and a Mushak 6.3 tax invoice is auto-generated on fulfilment. The A/R debit sits on A/R Aging with its due date until the customer pays (Dr Cash / Cr A/R). One fulfil action therefore resolves into six connected effects — stock out, reservation consumed, COGS, revenue, output VAT, and a receivable — every one traceable to this order.
